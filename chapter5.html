THE STRENGTH IN TRUTH

Chapter Five

The Gift of Boundaries

Morning sunlight rose over the city like a promise. Bobby sat outside the shelter with coffee in his hands and watched people come and go. Nothing around him looked different. Something inside him had changed.

He was no longer the man who automatically said yes.

Miss Ethel stepped outside carrying warmth in her smile.

"Morning, Bobby. You're up early."

He smiled.

"Been doin' a lot of thinkin'."

She laughed softly.

"That can be dangerous."

They sat together in comfortable silence. Then Bobby asked a question.

"Miss Ethel, you ever help people so much you forget how to help yourself?"

She stirred her coffee slowly.

"Boundaries ain't walls. Boundaries are doors."

"They tell people how to walk into your life and how to treat you."

Her words settled deeply. Grace said love needed wisdom. Miss Ethel said love needed boundaries. Truth kept arriving from every direction.

Later that afternoon Terry stumbled toward the shelter. Dirty. Hungry. Angry. Bobby had seen him before. Always asking for money. Always shouting.

Instinct told Bobby to reach into his pocket. This time he paused.

"Terry, I can get you food. I can't give you money."

Terry stared back. Confused. Angry.

"Man, you always help me."

Bobby stood calmly.

"I'll feed you. I'll talk with you. I ain't feedin' what's hurtin' you."

Silence stretched. Then Terry lowered his head.

"Alright man... I'll eat."

Later that night Miss Ethel smiled.

"You did good, Bobby."

For the first time in years Bobby felt something new. Strength. Not guilt. Not exhaustion. Strength.

Boundaries no longer felt selfish. Boundaries felt sacred.
