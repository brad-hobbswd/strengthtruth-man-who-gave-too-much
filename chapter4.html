THE STRENGTH IN TRUTH

Chapter Four

The Mirror of Mercy

That night Bobby could not sleep. He sat beneath the overpass while city lights flickered like memories. Grace's folded paper rested quietly in his pocket.

"Help me to love without losing myself."

Those words carried him backward through years of memories. Before the streets. Before loneliness. Before giving pieces of himself away.

Bobby once had a home. A job. A family. His mother Mary Lou raised him in a small Southern town.

She always told him:

"Bobby, do not ever turn your back on somebody in need."

He followed those words with his whole heart. Maybe too much.

After his mother passed away, Bobby kept helping anyone who asked. Especially his younger brother Carl.

Money. Rent. Food. Bail. Second chances.

Carl always promised things would change. Bobby always believed him.

Until one night Carl never came home.

Days later Bobby learned his brother overdosed in a motel room. Money meant for a fresh start became something else entirely.

Guilt followed Bobby for years afterward. Every yes became more than kindness. Every yes became an attempt to repair old pain.

"Mama... I did what you taught me. I just didn't know how to stop."

Tears rolled slowly down his face. He unfolded Grace's prayer again. This time he prayed aloud.

"Lord... teach me when to give and when to guard."

Something inside him began breaking. Not brokenness from pain. Brokenness before healing.

As dawn rose across the skyline, Bobby stared into a puddle. A reflection stared back. Weary. Tired. Still standing.

For the first time he saw more than a homeless man. He saw someone Jesus was still shaping.

Mercy showed him not what was gone. Mercy showed him what remained. Grace. Truth. Hope.
