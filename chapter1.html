THE STRENGTH IN TRUTH

Chapter One

The Streets and His Heart

The city’s night air carried the smell of rain and exhaust, blending with the faint hum of streetlights. Bobby sat on the edge of an old park bench with a small bag beside him.

Inside were the few things he owned, a folded blanket, a pair of socks, and a Bible with worn pages.

He was not completely without means. Once a month, a check came in, just enough to keep him fed and clothed.

He often said with a soft laugh:

"I don’t have bills, but I’ve got a heart that spends."

Every time that check arrived, so did the people. They came with stories, excuses, and needs that tugged on his kindness.

Bobby would share what little he had, buying meals, giving away clothes, even slipping money into hands that had already taken too much.

“Bobby, you're too good to folks,” said Miss Ethel, the shelter cook.

“They see that soft heart of yours and they take advantage.”

He smiled gently.

“I know, Miss Ethel. But helping folks helps me too. It reminds me I still got purpose.”

She sighed.

“There is helping, and then there is letting folks use you. You gotta learn the difference, baby.”

Her words stuck with him.

That night beneath the overpass, Bobby thought about all the faces he had helped. Some thankful. Most forgetful.

Love was not always about giving. Sometimes love was about saying no.

"Open rebuke is better than secret love."

He whispered: "So, you're saying... telling the truth can be love too?"

He did not know a single truth would soon test him in ways he never expected.
